Case study

Event-Driven Order Processing Platform

Retail · Microservices · Event-driven architecture

A modern order processing platform designed to handle peak traffic events through a microservices and messaging-based architecture.

Context

The client was a global retailer whose legacy monolithic order management system struggled during major sales campaigns. Spikes in traffic caused slow response times, duplicate orders and occasional downtime.

Non-functional requirements included handling 5x normal traffic, maintaining a highly available customer experience and improving observability across the order lifecycle.

Key drivers

  • Scale horizontally to handle spikes in order volume.
  • Isolate failures to prevent a single issue taking down the whole flow.
  • Improve visibility of order status across teams.

Solution

I designed a microservices architecture based on bounded contexts (Orders, Payments, Inventory, Notifications) communicating via an event bus/message broker.

Logical flow

  1. Order Service exposes an API used by web and mobile channels.
  2. On a valid order, it persists state and publishes an OrderCreated event.
  3. Payment Service consumes OrderCreated, calls external payment providers and emits OrderPaid or OrderPaymentFailed.
  4. Inventory Service listens to payment events, reserves or releases stock accordingly and emits StockReserved or StockFailed.
  5. Notification Service listens to business events and triggers emails/SMS updates to the customer.

Key design decisions

  • Event-driven integration: decoupled services and enabled them to scale independently, while smoothing load on downstream components.
  • Idempotent consumers: every consumer implemented idempotency based on business keys, ensuring safe handling of duplicate messages.
  • Resilience patterns: circuit breakers, retries with backoff and dead-letter queues used around external payment and notification providers.
  • Observability: correlation IDs and structured logging across services, plus metrics and dashboards for key business KPIs.

Outcomes

  • Successfully supported 5x traffic increase during major campaigns.
  • Reduced average order processing time by ~40% versus the previous system.
  • Improved fault isolation, reducing blast radius of issues.
