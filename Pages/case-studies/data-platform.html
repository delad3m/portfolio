Case study

Data Platform for Analytics & Reporting

Enterprise · Data lake · Analytics & BI

A central data platform that consolidates data from multiple systems to enable analytics, reporting and self-service insights.

Context

The organisation had multiple reporting solutions and data extracts maintained by different teams, leading to inconsistent numbers and high maintenance overhead.

The goal was to establish a governed, scalable platform for analytics and BI, while supporting both batch and near-real-time use cases.

Solution

I defined a layered data platform architecture, including ingestion, storage, curation and consumption layers, with clear responsibilities and data contracts.

Logical components

  • Ingestion: pipelines pulling data from source systems via CDC, file drops and APIs.
  • Storage: raw and curated zones in a cloud data lake.
  • Processing: batch and streaming jobs to clean, join and enrich data.
  • Serving: semantic models and curated datasets for BI tools and downstream consumers.

Key design decisions

  • Separation of raw and curated zones to allow reprocessing and maintain lineage.
  • Use of data contracts and ownership to align source and consuming teams.
  • Centralised monitoring of data pipeline health and data quality checks.

Outcomes

  • Reduced duplication of reporting solutions and conflicting numbers.
  • Improved time-to-insight for new analytical questions.
  • Clearer accountability for data domains and quality.
